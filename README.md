# 🏛️ What “Rome” truly is

It all commenced with Rome and the Roman Empire — they bequeathed unto posterity a **universal model of governance**:

* order and law;
* military might and control;
* the notion of “we are the centre of the world”.

👉 Thus, “Rome” transcended the bounds of a mere city and became an **archetype of empire**.

---

## 🌍 How the idea was cultivated

Every mighty realm has sought to proclaim:
**“We are the continuation of Rome.”**

* Constantinople → the “New Rome”;
* Moscow → the “Third Rome”;
* Paris and London → the imperial “Romes” of Europe;
* Washington → the “Rome of the Republic”.

Even Jerusalem is at times perceived as a **spiritual nexus**, verily a “celestial Rome”.

---

## 🦅 Why all emulate Rome

For it bestows:

* legitimacy (“we are ordained by history”);
* symbols of might (the eagle, the majestic columns, the senate);
* a sense of perpetuity.

👉 ’Tis far easier to adopt a proven model than to forge a new one anew.

---

## ⏳ Why all “Romes” inevitably fall (a recurring pattern)

Nigh unto invariably, the same sequence doth unfold:

1. **Ascent** — energy, vision, discipline;
2. **Zenith** — opulence and global dominion;
3. **Overextension** — a realm grown too vast to govern;
4. **Decadence of the elites** — corruption, internecine strife;
5. **Loss of the grand purpose** — the populace no longer believes in the “mission of Rome”;
6. **Crisis and decline**.

This fate hath befallen:

* the Roman Empire;
* the Byzantine Empire;
* other “heirs” who dared to assume the mantle.

---

## 💡 The summation in a single maxim

👉 “Rome” is a cycle:

**ascent → grandeur → overextension → fall**

And each new power:

* first emulates Rome;
* then becomes a “Rome” in its own right;
* and ultimately treads the self‑same path.
